# Extracted Product Information: Pure Himalayan Shilajit

## 1. Facts and Myths
- **Meaning**: "Shilajit" is derived from Sanskrit, meaning "conqueror of rocks or mountains".
- **Cognitive Benefits**: Ability to prevent tau protein buildup in the brain (Fontaine et al., 2024).
- **Mineral Content**: Contains more than 84 minerals including copper, silver, zinc, iron, and lead in their ionic forms (Ghosal & Lal, n.d).
- **Gender Benefits**: Offers numerous health benefits for both men and women, including improving overall health and cognitive function (not just for men's sexual health).

## 2. Formation Process
- **Description**: Shilajit is a natural substance ranging in color from pale brown to blackish-brown that exudes from rocks in mountain ranges, especially in the Himalayas.
- **Origin**: Formed for centuries from compressed plant materials under high pressure and temperature.
- **Exudation**: Due to the sun's heat on mountains, the Shilajit material seeps out of rock cracks.

## 3. Shilajit in Ayurveda
- **Historical Context**: Known as a special healer in Ayurveda, with roots tracing back to ancient texts like Charaka Samhita.
- **Healing Properties**: Known for healing a wide range of ailments and deformities. Ayurvedic writings emphasize that there is no disease or deformity that Shilajit cannot heal.

## 4. Where is it found?
- **Source**: Premium Himalayan Shilajit is sourced from the pristine Himalayan landscape of Nepal.
- **Specific Locations**: Dolpa, Mugu, Jajarkot, Humla, Rukum, Gorkha, etc.

## 5. Science behind Shilajit
- **Composition**: Rich in humic (80-85%) and non-humic (15-20%) compounds, including fulvic acid, dibenzo-a-pyrones, triterpenes, amino acids, and essential vitamins.
- **Process**: Formed through the humification of organic matter over centuries.
- **Nutrients**: Essential nutrients like vitamins B1 and B12 contribute to its antioxidant properties and therapeutic efficacy by enhancing cellular energy and combating oxidative stress (Wilson et al., 2011).
